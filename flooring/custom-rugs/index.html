Custom area rugs are one of the most useful — and most overlooked — things a real flooring shop can make for you. Instead of hunting for a rug that's almost the right size or nearly the right shade, you choose the carpet you love, tell us the dimensions of your room, and we finish the edges into a clean, durable area rug built for your space. It's a small, local service most national flooring chains and big-box stores simply don't offer, and it's a favorite of Carson City, Reno and Sparks homeowners who want something that fits the room exactly.

How custom rugs work

The process is refreshingly simple, and it starts in our Carson City showroom. You pick a carpet — from a roll, a sample line, or even a leftover remnant from a recent install — and we measure the size you need. From there our team cuts the carpet square (or to a runner, oval, or other shape) and finishes every raw edge so it won't fray or unravel underfoot. The result is a finished rug, not a piece of cut carpet.

  1. Choose your carpet. Browse styles, colors and fibers in the showroom, or repurpose a quality remnant you already have.
  2. Give us the size. Bring your room dimensions (or have us measure during a free in-home visit) so the rug lands exactly where you want it.
  3. Pick your edge. Select cloth binding or a yarn-wrapped serged edge, plus a color that blends in or stands out.
  4. We fabricate & finish. We cut, bind and inspect the rug, then it's ready to pick up or have delivered.

Edges, binding & materials

The edge is what turns carpet into a rug, and you have choices. Cloth (fabric-tape) binding wraps the perimeter in a fabric band for a crisp, tailored border — pick a tape color that matches the carpet for an invisible finish or a contrasting one to frame the piece. Serging wraps the edge in tightly stitched yarn for a softer, hand-finished look that reads more like a traditional area rug. We'll walk you through binding widths and colors against your actual carpet so the finished edge looks intentional, not improvised.

Because the rug is made from real carpet, you also get to choose the fiber and feel — soft nylon or polyester for living spaces and bedrooms, denser low-pile constructions for high-traffic entries and hallways, or wool-blend lines when you want a natural, premium hand. Our high-desert climate is gentle on rugs indoors, but for entryways that catch grit and snowmelt in a Northern Nevada winter, we'll steer you toward a tighter, more cleanable construction.

Where custom rugs work best

A made-to-measure rug solves problems a store-bought one can't. Use one to define a seating area over a wide expanse of hardwood or luxury vinyl, to soften and quiet a room without wall-to-wall carpet, or to protect the floor in front of a sofa, under a dining table, or in a play area. Long, narrow runners are perfect for hallways and entries, and a custom rug lets you carry one carpet color from a carpeted bedroom out into an adjoining hard-surface room for a cohesive look. If you're carpeting part of the home, ask about turning the same carpet into matching area rugs — it's an easy way to tie rooms together.

Bound rugs from carpet remnants

One of the best-kept secrets of working with a local store is the remnant rack. After larger installations we're often left with quality carpet remnants, and those pieces make excellent bound carpet rugs at a friendly price. Have leftover carpet from a past project of your own? Bring it in — in most cases we can bind it into a finished rug for you. It's a practical, lower-waste way to get a one-of-a-kind rug sized for your room.

Custom rug FAQ

What sizes and shapes can you make?
We make rugs to your exact measurements — from small accent rugs to large room-size pieces, plus long hallway runners. Rectangles and squares are most common, and we can often accommodate other shapes; bring your room dimensions and we'll confirm what's possible with the carpet you choose.
Can you bind carpet I already have?
In most cases, yes. If you have a sound carpet remnant from a previous project, bring it to our Carson City showroom and we can typically finish the edges into a bound or serged area rug. We'll take a look first and let you know honestly whether your piece is a good candidate.
How long does a custom rug take?
Turnaround depends on the carpet, the size and the edge finish you choose. Many rugs are completed within a short window once the material is on hand; we'll give you a realistic timeline when you place your order so there are no surprises.
What's the difference between binding and serging?
Binding wraps the edge in a fabric tape for a clean, tailored border, while serging wraps it in stitched yarn for a softer, more traditional rug look. Both keep the edge from fraying — the choice is mostly about the style you want. We'll show you each against your carpet.
