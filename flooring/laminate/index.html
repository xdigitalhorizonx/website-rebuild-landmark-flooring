Laminate flooring remains one of the best values on the market in Carson City and across Northern Nevada — a tough, photo-realistic wood-look floor that costs far less than solid hardwood and stands up beautifully to daily traffic. Today's laminate looks nothing like the shiny, hollow boards of twenty years ago: high-definition print layers, deep embossed textures and beveled plank edges give it convincing depth, while a hard wear surface fends off the scuffs, sun and scratches a real high-desert home throws at it. What is laminate — and how it differs from LVP

Laminate is built around a dense high-density fiberboard (HDF) core made from compressed wood fibers. On top sits a printed decorative layer and a clear, aluminum-oxide wear layer; underneath is a stabilizing backing. Boards click together and "float" over an underlayment — they're never glued or nailed to the subfloor — which makes for a fast, clean install. Laminate's wear resistance is rated on the AC scale: AC3 suits typical residential rooms, while AC4–AC5 handles heavier traffic and light commercial use.

The key thing to understand is the difference between laminate and luxury vinyl. Because laminate's core is wood-based, standard laminate is water-resistant, not waterproof — it tolerates quick spills wiped up promptly, but standing water and high-moisture rooms can swell the core over time. Newer water-resistant laminate lines add tighter seals and improve on this, yet for kitchens, full baths, laundry rooms and basements we usually steer customers to waterproof luxury vinyl instead. If you're weighing the two head-to-head, our LVP vs laminate vs hardwood guide breaks down exactly where each one wins.

Styles & looks

Laminate spans a huge range of wood looks — from pale, on-trend white oak and weathered gray to warm hickory and rich espresso walnut. Wider, longer planks with low-gloss matte finishes read as the most natural and current, and embossed-in-register (EIR) textures align the surface grain with the printed grain for a genuinely wood-like feel underfoot. Beveled edges add definition between boards and help hide the slight unevenness common in older Carson City homes. In our showroom you can handle real samples side by side before you commit.

Where laminate works best

Laminate is a natural fit for dry, everyday living spaces: living and family rooms, dining rooms, bedrooms, home offices and hallways. Those are exactly the rooms where Northern Nevada's low humidity actually works in laminate's favor — there's far less ambient moisture to threaten the core than you'd find in a humid coastal climate. It's also a favorite for budget-minded whole-home refreshes and for landlords updating a rental on a timeline, because it goes down quickly and wears like iron in high-traffic zones.

Where we'd point you elsewhere: any room that sees standing water or steam. For those, lean on waterproof luxury vinyl. And if soft, quiet warmth underfoot matters most in bedrooms, compare laminate with our other flooring options.

Cost & pricing guidance

Laminate is firmly in the budget category, which is a big part of its appeal. Installed, it typically runs in the general range of $3–$7 per square foot for materials and professional installation, depending on the board's thickness and AC rating, your subfloor's condition, and extras like old-floor removal, underlayment upgrades, stairs and transition trim. These are general industry estimates, not a Landmark quote — every room is different, which is why our estimates are always free. Laminate flooring FAQ

Is laminate flooring waterproof?
Standard laminate is water-resistant, not waterproof — it handles quick spills wiped up promptly, but its wood-based core can swell with standing water or in high-moisture rooms. Some newer water-resistant laminate lines perform better, but for kitchens, full baths, laundry rooms and basements we recommend waterproof luxury vinyl instead.
How long does laminate flooring last?
A quality laminate installed correctly in the right rooms commonly lasts 15–25 years. Choosing a higher AC rating for busy areas, keeping standing water off it, and using the right underlayment all extend its life. The dry Northern Nevada climate is generally kind to laminate's wood-based core.
Can you install laminate over my existing floor?
Often, yes — because laminate floats, it can frequently go over an existing hard, sound, flat surface with the right underlayment. We always assess the subfloor first; some floors need leveling or removal for the best long-term result, and we'll tell you honestly what yours needs in your free estimate.
Is laminate cheaper than vinyl or hardwood?
Laminate is typically the most budget-friendly wood-look option, generally running less than solid hardwood and competitive with many luxury vinyl lines. For a full local breakdown, see our flooring cost guide — and remember the figures there are estimates, not a quote.
