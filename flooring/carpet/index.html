Carpet types & fibers

Carpet character comes down to two things: how the yarn is constructed and what the yarn is made from. Getting both right for the room is where a real carpet near me search should pay off — and it's the part big-box counters tend to rush.

Pile construction

  • Cut pile (plush & Saxony): yarn tips are sheared for a soft, even, formal surface. The most luxurious feel, ideal for bedrooms and formal living rooms.
  • Loop & Berber: yarn loops left uncut create a low, dense, hard-wearing surface that hides footprints and vacuum marks — a smart pick for stairs, halls and family rooms.
  • Frieze (twist): tightly twisted strands that curl in different directions, giving a casual textured look that masks tracks and everyday wear in high-traffic zones.
  • Cut-and-loop patterns: a mix of cut and looped yarn that builds in subtle sculpted patterns and added durability.

Fiber options

  • Nylon: the most resilient mainstream fiber — it springs back from foot traffic and furniture, making it the go-to for stairs and busy households.
  • Polyester (PET): luxuriously soft and naturally stain-resistant with rich color, a great value for bedrooms and lower-traffic rooms.
  • Triexta: a newer fiber prized for built-in, permanent stain resistance and durability — excellent for homes with pets and kids.
  • Wool: the natural premium option, soft and beautiful, often chosen for area definition or formal spaces.

Many of today's carpets pair a durable fiber with a factory-applied stain-resistant and soil-release treatment, so an accidental spill blots up instead of setting in. Best rooms for carpet

Carpet earns its place wherever comfort and quiet matter most. The clearest wins:

  • Bedrooms: the classic carpet room — warm on bare feet first thing on a cold Carson City morning, and soft enough for the best carpet for living room and bedroom comfort budgets alike.
  • Family & living rooms: a soft floor invites people to sit, play and gather; a textured frieze or cut-and-loop hides everyday traffic.
  • Stairs & hallways: carpet adds critical grip and absorbs the sound of feet on the stairs — choose a tough, low-profile construction here.
  • Bonus rooms & basements: instant warmth and acoustic dampening for media rooms and play spaces.

For entryways, kitchens, baths and homes with frequent pet accidents, a waterproof hard surface is usually the better call — compare our luxury vinyl for those zones, or mix the two so each room gets the right floor.

Durability, stain resistance & pets

How long a carpet looks new depends on matching its traffic rating to the room. A higher-rated, densely tufted carpet in a resilient fiber will outlast a thinner one in the same hallway by years. For Northern Nevada's dry, dusty air, a denser pile also traps airborne dust until you vacuum it away.

If you have pets or young kids, lean toward solution-dyed nylon or triexta with a strong factory stain treatment — the color goes all the way through the fiber, so it resists fading, bleaching and staining from accidents. Pair it with a quality moisture-resistant pad for the best defense. Carpet is wonderfully comfortable, but it isn't waterproof; if pet accidents are a daily reality, read our best flooring for pets and kids guide to weigh carpet against waterproof options room by room.

Carpet FAQ

What's the most durable carpet for a busy household?
For stairs, halls and family rooms, a densely tufted nylon or triexta in a loop, Berber or frieze construction holds up best — the resilient fiber springs back from traffic and the textured surface hides footprints. We'll match the traffic rating to each room during your free estimate.
Is carpet a good idea if I have pets?
It can be, with the right choice: solution-dyed nylon or triexta with a factory stain treatment resists accidents and fading, and a moisture-resistant pad adds protection. That said, carpet isn't waterproof — if accidents are frequent, see our best flooring for pets and kids guide to compare it with waterproof options.
Do you remove and haul away my old carpet?
Yes. Tear-out and haul-away of your existing carpet and pad are part of a typical installation, and we leave the room clean. We'll confirm the scope and any subfloor prep in your written estimate so there are no surprises.
How long does carpet installation take?
Most homes are installed in a single day, depending on square footage, stairs and how much furniture needs moving. We'll give you a realistic timeline when we measure your space.
