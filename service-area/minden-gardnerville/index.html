Flooring built for Carson Valley homes & the high-desert climate

The Carson Valley is a mix of housing eras, and the right floor depends on which corner of it you call home. We install through downtown Minden and historic downtown Gardnerville, where older homes often have original subfloors that reward careful prep; across the sprawling Gardnerville Ranchos, with everything from starter homes to custom ranchettes; and out on the larger lots of Johnson Lane, the Pine Nut and Buckeye East Valley, and horse and ranch properties where mudrooms and high-traffic entries take a beating. Newer subdivisions and estate neighborhoods like Chichester and the valley-floor ranchettes get the same attention. Whatever the era of your house, we match the material to how each room is really used.

Minden and Gardnerville sit around 4,700 feet on the Carson Valley floor, sheltered between the Carson Range of the Sierra to the west and the Pine Nut Mountains to the east. That means a true high-desert climate: dry, hot summers, cold winters with real snow (the valley averages roughly 22 inches a year), and big day-to-night temperature swings year-round. Dry indoor air — made drier still by winter heating — pulls moisture out of solid wood, which is exactly why so many Carson Valley homeowners notice gapping between solid hardwood boards by late winter. It's avoidable. We often steer valley clients toward engineered hardwood (far more dimensionally stable) or waterproof luxury vinyl, and when solid wood is the goal we insist on proper acclimation before a single board goes down. For the full reasoning, see our guide to the best flooring for Northern Nevada's climate.

My hardwood floors keep gapping in winter — what should I do?
That's the classic symptom of the Carson Valley's dry, high-desert air pulling moisture from solid wood, and winter heating makes it worse. Options range from running a humidifier, to choosing more stable engineered hardwood, to switching to waterproof luxury vinyl. We'll assess your floor in person and recommend what genuinely fits your home and budget.

Absolutely — from a single room to an entire house, including mudrooms, high-traffic entries, stairs, old-floor removal and subfloor prep. We schedule around your household and haul away the old material when we're done.
