Flooring built for Reno homes & the high-desert climate

Reno's neighborhoods are wonderfully varied, and the right floor depends on which one you're in. We install across Midtown and the Old Southwest, where older bungalows and mid-century homes often have original subfloors that reward careful prep; out in Somersett, Caughlin Ranch and the Northwest, where larger newer homes call for whole-floor consistency; and through South Reno, Damonte Ranch and the rest of the south valley's growing subdivisions. Whatever the era of your house, we match the material to how the room is actually used.

Reno sits in the rain shadow of the Sierra at roughly 4,500 feet, and that high-desert air is dry — single-digit humidity is common in summer, and indoor heating drops it further in winter. Dry air pulls moisture out of solid wood, which is exactly why so many Reno homeowners see gapping between solid hardwood boards by late winter. It's the most common flooring complaint we hear in the valley, and it's avoidable. We often steer Reno clients toward engineered hardwood (far more dimensionally stable) or waterproof luxury vinyl, and when solid wood is the goal we insist on proper acclimation before a single board goes down. For the full reasoning, see our guide to the best flooring for Northern Nevada's climate.

My hardwood floors keep gapping in winter — what should I do in Reno?
That's the classic symptom of Reno's dry, high-desert air pulling moisture from solid wood. Options range from running a humidifier, to choosing more stable engineered hardwood, to switching to waterproof luxury vinyl. We'll assess your floor in person and recommend what genuinely fits your home and budget.

Absolutely — from a single room to an entire house, including old-floor removal, subfloor prep, stairs and transitions. We schedule around your household and haul away the old material when we're done.
