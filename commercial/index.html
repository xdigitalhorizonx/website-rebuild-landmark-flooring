A commercial floor is a working surface, not just a finish. It absorbs rolling loads, foot traffic, dropped tools, spilled coffee and daily cleaning — and when it fails, it takes part of your business offline with it. That's why facility managers, building owners, contractors and business owners across Carson City, Reno, Sparks and the Carson Valley bring their projects to Landmark Flooring: we treat the floor as part of your operation, specify products rated for the wear it will actually see, and run the install so your doors stay open.

Why businesses choose Landmark for commercial work

Buying commercial flooring is a different conversation than choosing a floor for a living room. You're weighing wear ratings, slip resistance, maintenance cost over years, warranty terms, and — above all — how much disruption the install causes. Landmark is built for that conversation:

  • One accountable local team. We sell the material and install it with our own crews, so there's no finger-pointing between a flooring rep and a separate labor sub when something needs to be made right.
  • Minimal downtime. We phase work, install in off-hours or in sections, and sequence around your operations so a sales floor, office or clinic keeps running while the work gets done.
  • Specified for the use. A medical waiting room, a warehouse breakroom and a boutique retail floor all need different products — we match the wear layer, backing and finish to the actual traffic and cleaning regimen.
  • Licensed, bonded & insured. Essential for working in occupied commercial buildings and satisfying GCs, landlords and property owners.
  • Local and responsive. Our showroom and crews are based right here in Carson City — not dispatched from out of state — so site visits, change orders and punch-list items get handled fast.

Materials best suited to commercial spaces

Most commercial projects come down to a handful of hard-working product categories. We'll help you choose based on traffic, moisture, acoustics, budget and how easily a damaged area can be repaired down the road.

MaterialBest forWhy it works commercially
Commercial LVT / rigid-core vinylOffices, retail, clinics, hospitalityWaterproof, thick wear layer, wood- and stone-looks, individual planks can be swapped if damaged.

Many commercial clients pair commercial luxury vinyl in entries, corridors and wet areas with carpet tile in offices and meeting rooms for comfort and acoustics. We'll walk the space and recommend the right mix.

How a commercial project runs

Commercial work lives or dies on coordination. Here's how we keep a project predictable from first call to final walk-through.

  1. Site walk & estimate. We visit the space, measure, assess the subfloor and existing flooring, and document conditions that affect cost and schedule.
  2. Spec & proposal. We recommend products by wear rating and budget, then provide a clear written quote covering material, removal, prep and labor.
  3. Schedule around operations. We lock in phasing — after-hours, weekends, or section-by-section — so your business keeps functioning during install.
  4. Install & subfloor prep. Our crews handle moisture and flatness checks, prep, layout, transitions and trim to commercial-grade standards.
  5. Walk-through & haul-away. We clean up, remove old materials and debris, and walk the finished floor with you to close out the punch list.

Budgeting your commercial floor

Commercial pricing depends heavily on scope: square footage, the product grade, how much existing flooring and adhesive must be removed, subfloor prep and moisture mitigation, after-hours scheduling, and site access. Because those variables swing widely between a small office refresh and a multi-room build-out, we don't publish flat commercial rates — every project gets a written quote after a site walk. Any figures we discuss before measuring are general estimates, not a Landmark quote. For repeat or volume work, ask about account pricing.

Commercial flooring FAQ

Can you install while our business stays open?
Usually, yes. We phase commercial installs around your operations — after hours, on weekends, or one section at a time — so you can keep serving customers or staff while the work progresses. We'll build the phasing plan into the proposal.
What's the most durable flooring for a high-traffic commercial space?
For most offices, retail and clinics, commercial-grade rigid-core LVT with a thick wear layer is the workhorse — waterproof, tough, and repairable plank-by-plank. Modular carpet tile is the go-to where acoustics and comfort matter. We match the product to your specific traffic and cleaning routine.
Do you work with general contractors and property owners on tenant improvements?
Yes. We work as the flooring scope on TI and build-out projects, coordinate with the GC's schedule, and provide the licensing and insurance documentation building owners and landlords require.
Do you handle ongoing or volume work, like multiple units or locations?
We do. For property managers and businesses with recurring needs, we can set up account pricing and repeatable scheduling. See our property management flooring page, or ask us when you request a quote.
