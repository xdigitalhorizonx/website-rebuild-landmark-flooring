Our installation process

Every project — a single bedroom or a whole house — runs through the same five steps so there are no surprises on install day.

1

Measure & estimate

We measure your space (in-home or from your plans), talk through how each room is used, and give you a clear written estimate at no charge.

2

Choose your floor

Compare samples in our Carson City showroom and preview them in your own room with the in-store visualizer, then lock in materials and a date.

3

Prep & acclimate

We remove the old floor, assess and prep the subfloor for flatness and moisture, and acclimate wood and rigid-core products to your home's conditions.

4

The skilled installers on our team set the floor to manufacturer spec — proper layout, seams, expansion gaps, and clean transitions and trim throughout.

5

Clean up & haul away

We clean the work area and haul away the old flooring and debris, then walk the finished job with you before we leave.

Why prep matters in Northern Nevada

Our high-desert climate is hard on floors that aren't installed correctly. Carson City and the Reno valleys swing from arid summer heat to freezing winter nights with very low humidity, which makes solid wood shrink and gap if it isn't acclimated and given the right expansion room. Up around Tahoe, snowmelt and moisture push the priorities the other way. A floor installed by people who understand these conditions behaves; one rushed in by an out-of-area crew often doesn't. Our skilled installers prep and acclimate for the local environment, and our guide to the best flooring for Northern Nevada's climate explains the why in plain terms.

What affects your installation price

There's no honest flat rate for flooring installation, because the work hiding under and around your floor varies house to house. The factors that move a quote most are:

  • Square footage & layout: total area, room count, closets and tight cuts.
  • Old-floor removal & haul-away: tearing out and disposing of existing flooring and pad.
  • Subfloor condition: leveling, moisture mitigation or repairs add labor.
  • Stairs & transitions: staircases, thresholds and trim are detail-heavy work.
  • Material choice: carpet, laminate, LVP and hardwood each install differently.

For ballpark figures by material, our flooring cost guide for Carson City & Northern Nevada lays out general ranges — all labeled as estimates, not a Landmark quote. The only way to get a real number is a quick measure of your space, which is exactly what a free estimate gives you.

Flooring installation FAQ

Do you install floors I bought somewhere else?
We focus on installing the flooring we supply, because that's how The Landmark Guarantee can cover both the product and the labor with no finger-pointing. If you've already bought material elsewhere, call us and we'll talk through your options honestly.
Who installs my floor?
The skilled installers on our team measure, prep and install your floor — they're part of Landmark, which is why we can warranty our workmanship directly.
How long does a flooring installation take?
It depends on the material and square footage. A single room can often be done in a day; a whole-home install, or a job that needs subfloor prep, hardwood acclimation or refinishing cure time, takes longer. We give you a realistic timeline in your written estimate before any work starts.
Do you move furniture and remove the old floor?
Yes — old-floor removal and haul-away are part of what we quote, and we'll discuss furniture handling with you up front so install day runs smoothly. These factors are included in your estimate.
