Why proper carpet installation matters

Carpet looks deceptively simple to lay, which is exactly why so many bargain installs fail early. The two things that separate a lasting install from a callback are how the carpet is stretched and how the seams and pad are handled.

Power-stretching, not just a knee-kicker

A knee-kicker alone can't pull carpet tight enough across a full room. We use a power stretcher to put real, even tension on the carpet so it grips the tack strip and stays flat. Skipping this is the number-one reason carpet "grows" wrinkles and ripples a year or two in — and re-stretching later costs you money you shouldn't have to spend. Tight installation also helps the carpet wear evenly, which matters in the dry Northern Nevada climate where grit tracked in from gravel drives and trails grinds at loose fibers.

Smart seam placement & the right pad

Seams are unavoidable in rooms wider than a roll, but where they land is a craft decision. We place seams away from the main walking path and direct light so they practically disappear, then heat-seam them for a strong, lasting bond. Underneath, the carpet pad does the quiet work: the right density and thickness protects your carpet's backing, adds comfort and sound absorption, and is required to keep most manufacturer warranties valid. We never reuse old, flattened pad.

Carpet installation FAQ

Do you remove and haul away my old carpet?
Yes. Standard carpet installations include tearing out the old carpet, pad and tack strip where needed and hauling it all away, so you're left with a finished room and a clean job site. We'll confirm the scope in your free estimate.
Do I need to move my furniture first?
Light clearing helps, and emptying rooms entirely is ideal for the cleanest install. For heavier pieces, ask about our move-and-replace option when you book — we'll let you know what's involved and price it up front so there are no surprises on install day.
Why does carpet need to be power-stretched?
A power stretcher applies even, room-wide tension that a knee-kicker can't match. Properly stretched carpet stays flat and tight, resists rippling, and wears more evenly — it's the single biggest factor in whether your carpet still looks right years from now.
Can you carpet just my stairs?
Absolutely. Stairs are one of the most common carpet jobs we do, and they're a place where skilled hand-work really shows. Each tread and riser is fitted and secured individually for a snug, safe, long-lasting result.
