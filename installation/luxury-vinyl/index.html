What's included in a Landmark LVP/LVT install

Our luxury vinyl installations are turnkey. You don't hire one company to tear out the old floor, another to prep, and a third to lay the planks — one crew owns the whole job from the first measurement to the final haul-away.

  • On-site measure & layout planning: we measure every room, plan plank direction for the best sightlines and the fewest seams, and stage material to acclimate.
  • Old-floor removal: tear-out and disposal of existing carpet, vinyl, tile or laminate when needed.
  • Subfloor prep: the step that makes or breaks a vinyl floor — flattening high and low spots, securing squeaks, and checking moisture.
  • Precise installation: click-lock floating install (or glue-down where a product calls for it), with consistent perimeter expansion gaps.
  • Transitions & trim: clean thresholds between rooms and flooring types, plus baseboard or quarter-round to finish the edges.
  • Haul-away & cleanup: we leave the space swept, walkable and ready to live on.

Most single-level homes are installed in one to three working days once material is on site — larger or multi-room jobs run longer, and we'll give you a realistic timeline with your estimate so you can plan around it.

Why installation makes or breaks waterproof performance

The biggest myth about luxury vinyl is that "waterproof" means foolproof. The plank itself won't swell from a spill, but a floating LVP system still relies on a handful of details that only a careful installer gets right:

What proper install delivers

  • A flat subfloor so locking seams stay tight and don't peak or gap
  • Correct perimeter expansion gaps so the floor can move with temperature swings
  • Properly sealed transitions at doorways, baths and exterior doors
  • Manufacturer install specs followed so the product warranty stays valid

What rushed installs leave behind

  • Telegraphing — every dip or bump in the subfloor showing through the planks
  • Buckling or peaked seams when no expansion gap was left
  • Water sneaking under planks at sloppy doorway or tub transitions

Northern Nevada's high-desert climate makes the expansion-gap step matter even more here than in milder regions: a Carson City or Reno living room can swing from baking summer afternoons to freezing winter nights, and a floating vinyl floor needs room to move with it. Our skilled installers size gaps to the product and the room so your floor stays flat and quiet year-round.

Luxury vinyl installation FAQ

Can you install luxury vinyl over my existing floor?
Often, yes — rigid-core LVP and LVT can frequently float over an existing hard surface if it's sound, clean and flat. We always assess your subfloor first; some floors (especially failing tile, cushioned vinyl or very uneven surfaces) are better removed or prepped, and we'll tell you honestly which path gives the best long-term result during your free estimate.
Do you have to remove the old flooring?
Not always. When the existing floor is flat and stable, we may install right over it; when it isn't, removal and disposal are part of the scope. Either way, tear-out and haul-away are spelled out in your written estimate so there are no surprises on install day.
Why does the subfloor matter so much for vinyl?
Luxury vinyl is relatively thin and flexible, so it conforms to whatever is beneath it. Dips, bumps and debris telegraph through as visible waves or cause locking seams to fail. Flattening and prepping the subfloor is the single biggest difference between a floor that looks great for years and one that disappoints — it's a step the skilled installers on our team never skip.
How long does an LVP installation take?
Most single-level homes are done in one to three working days once material has acclimated on site. Heavy subfloor prep, stairs, multiple rooms or large square footage add time. We give you a realistic timeline with your estimate so you can plan around the work.
