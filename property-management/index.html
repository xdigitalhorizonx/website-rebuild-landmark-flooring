When a unit goes vacant, every day between move-out and move-in is lost rent — so the floor has to be specified, scheduled and installed without the back-and-forth. Landmark Flooring works the way managers and landlords need us to: we recommend the right durable product for rental wear, quote it at volume-friendly pricing, and send the skilled installers on our team to get it down quickly so you can re-list the unit and re-key the door.

Best flooring for rental properties

For the vast majority of rental turns we recommend waterproof rigid-core luxury vinyl plank (LVP). It is, frankly, the workhorse of rental flooring: it's 100% waterproof so tenant spills and pet accidents don't destroy it, it resists the scratches and dents that come with constant move-in/move-out traffic, and it cleans up with a damp mop between residents. Rigid-core LVP also stays dimensionally stable through Northern Nevada's dry high-desert summers and cold valley winters, which means fewer call-backs for gapping or warping down the road.

Carpet still earns its place in bedrooms and on stairs where tenants want warmth and quiet underfoot, and it's an economical choice for lower-traffic units. We'll help you choose a stain-resistant line and the right pad so it holds up turn after turn. Explore the products we most often spec for rentals on our luxury vinyl and carpet pages, and see how the materials compare in our LVP vs. laminate vs. hardwood guide.

Pricing & what affects a turn

Turn pricing depends on the unit: square footage, how much old flooring has to come up and be hauled away, stairs and transitions, and the product grade you choose. As a general industry reference, professionally installed rigid-core LVP commonly falls in the $4–$9 per square foot range for materials and labor. That's a general estimate, not a Landmark quote — volume turn work is priced differently than a single retail order, which is exactly why managers ask us for a portfolio rate. Send us your unit details for real numbers, and ask about financing for larger rollouts.

Property management flooring FAQ

Do you offer volume or portfolio pricing for property managers?
Yes. Repeat turn work and standardized rollouts are priced differently than a one-off retail order. Tell us about your portfolio and the products you want specced, and we'll set up consistent pricing you can re-order against without re-quoting each unit.
What's the most durable, budget-friendly floor for a rental?
For most turns we recommend waterproof rigid-core luxury vinyl plank. It's waterproof, scratch- and dent-resistant, easy to clean between tenants, and stable through Northern Nevada's dry climate — which keeps future call-backs down. Carpet still works well in bedrooms and on stairs.
How fast can you turn a vacant unit?
It depends on unit size, scope and our current schedule, but we build our turn process around speed because we know vacancy costs you rent. Send us the address and access details and we'll give you an honest timeline — we won't promise a date we can't hit.
Do you handle single units or only large portfolios?
Both. Whether you manage a handful of doors or a large portfolio, a single turn gets the same quick scheduling. Larger and repeat accounts simply unlock more predictable pricing and faster re-orders.
